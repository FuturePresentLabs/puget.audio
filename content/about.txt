Puget Audio builds audio tools for musicians who love analog and still have to ship tracks.

We love analog. In 2026 the iron is expensive, scarce, and not always practical. Our goal is simple: hand you the sound — the saturation, the dynamics, the little character things — without the rack, the reshell, or the insurance rider.

So we do two things.

We simulate analog when the math gets us closer than a capture. Our plugins model actual circuit physics — resistors, capacitors, tubes, transformers — in real time. When you turn a knob, you're changing a component value in a simulated circuit, the same way the original hardware works.

We sample analog when nothing else will do. Some sounds only exist on the hardware. When that's true, we hook up the real synth, print 24-bit / 48 kHz through a real signal chain, and put the gear we used right on the box.

We also build a little hardware of our own — Eurorack cases, in Seattle, in small batches, in the colors you actually want.

Based in Seattle. Rooted in open technology.

Built by people who play the stuff they make.
