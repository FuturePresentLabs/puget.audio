Why Legends?

Some pedals became legendary because guitarists couldn’t stop chasing their sound. Originals sell for thousands. Clones miss the point. Captures freeze one setting in amber.

We went back to the schematics and rebuilt each circuit at the component level—so you get the real behavior, not an approximation of it. Every knob interaction. Every cleanup. Every weird, beautiful thing the circuit does that the designers didn’t plan.

What Makes This Different

It cleans up like the real thing.

Roll back your guitar volume and GOLDENROD transitions from overdrive to clean boost. AUSTIN goes from biting treble boost to warm, clean sparkle. This is the single biggest thing every other pedal plugin gets wrong. We get it right because we model the actual circuit interaction between your guitar’s output impedance and the pedal’s input stage.

It responds like the real thing.

Pick dynamics, string attack, volume knob position—the circuit responds to all of it because it’s processing signal through simulated components, not looking up a pre-baked response curve. MUFF’s sustain blooms and breathes. RATKING’s filter sweeps interact with the gain stage. The feel is the physics.

It can be modded like the real thing.

Open circuit view. Swap diode types in GOLDENROD. Change the transistor in AUSTIN. Push MUFF’s sustain into infinite feedback. Every mod that exists on a forum thread, available in one click—plus mods that would destroy the original hardware. Your pedal. Your circuit. Your rules.

The Lineup

GOLDENROD

“The pedal that started a religion.”

Transparent Overdrive · Clean Boost · Edge of Breakup

Dual germanium diode clipping, clean blend circuit, charge pump voltage doubler. The full gain-knob interaction that captures and clones flatten into a single snapshot.

Modeled After: Klon Centaur

RATKING

“The filter is the voice.”

Distortion · Fuzz-Adjacent Saturation · Shoegaze Wall

LM308 op-amp hard clipping into the legendary passive filter network. First published WDF model of this circuit—the filter-gain interaction solved from the schematic.

Modeled After: ProCo RAT

MUFF

“The wall of sound.”

Fuzz · Sustain · Crushing Distortion

Four-stage clipping with variable tone stack. The sustain control pushes the circuit from articulate fuzz into infinite, blooming distortion. Models the complete signal path including the tone circuit’s midrange scoop and the unique character of the clipping stages.

Modeled After: Big Muff Pi

AUSTIN

“The treble that cuts through the mix.”

Treble Booster · Edge of Breakup · British Tone

Single germanium transistor with a high-pass filter network. The secret weapon of British blues and hard rock guitarists. Pushes your amp into sweet, singing overdrive while retaining clarity and bite. The tone that defined an era.

Modeled After: Dallas Rangemaster

SCREAMER

“The midrange hump that cuts.”

Overdrive · Boost · Amp Pusher

Single op-amp with symmetrical clipping and the legendary passive tone control. The famous midrange emphasis pushes your signal through any mix. Models the complete circuit including the input buffer, gain stage, and the tone stack’s unique frequency response.

Modeled After: Tube Screamer
