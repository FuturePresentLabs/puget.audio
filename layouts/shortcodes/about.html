What we do

Analog, three ways.

One philosophy: honest sound at a fair price. Three paths to it — simulation, sampling, and small-batch hardware that keeps real iron in the room.

{{/* ---------- Feature 1 — left aligned ---------- */}}
01
Simulate

We model the circuit, not the sound of it.

A capture freezes one moment. A profile bakes in one setting. Our plugins do neither. We rebuild the schematic in real time — every resistor, capacitor, tube, and transformer simulated as a physical object that reacts to your signal. Change a knob, you’re changing a component value. Dig in harder, the circuit pushes back. All of it is powered by our open-source PedalKernel engine.

  • Wave Digital Filter engine, component-accurate
  • Preserves the dynamic “feel” of the original hardware
  • No presets required — the knobs are the circuit
{{/* ---------- Feature 2 — right aligned ---------- */}}
02
Sample

When only the hardware will do, we record it.

Some sounds don’t reduce to a schematic. A Moog DFAM screaming into an Eventide. A SoundToys chain into a tape saturator. For these, math isn’t the shortcut — the room is. We track source material through the actual gear, in Seattle, at 24-bit / 48 kHz, with artist-grade preamps and no AI cleanup.

  • Recorded in our Seattle room — mic’d, DI’d, and chained
  • 24-bit / 48 kHz WAV, royalty-free for your productions
  • MPC expansions & Ableton-ready where it matters

03
Build

And some tools have to be real.

Software solves a lot. Samples cover the rest. But some problems need a physical box — real knobs, real power, real space on your desk. So we make our own, small-batch, built to order in Seattle and priced like you’re the one ordering it. Starting with the Cornucopia Eurorack case today. DSP hardware is next on the bench, and the analog gear that inspired the plugins will follow.

  • Designed, cut, and finished in our Seattle shop
  • Honest pricing — no collector-market markup
  • Built on open standards so it plays with the rest of your rig
