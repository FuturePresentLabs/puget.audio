Why Legends?

Some pedals became legendary because guitarists couldn’t stop chasing their sound. Originals sell for thousands. Clones miss the point. Captures freeze one setting in amber.

We went back to the schematics and rebuilt each circuit at the component level—so you get the real behavior, not an approximation of it. Every knob interaction. Every cleanup. Every weird, beautiful thing the circuit does that the designers didn’t plan.

{{/* ======================================== WHAT MAKES THIS DIFFERENT ======================================== */}}

What Makes This Different

It cleans up like the real thing.

Roll back your guitar volume and GOLDENROD transitions from overdrive to clean boost. FIZZ goes from fuzz to sparkling clean. This is the single biggest thing every other pedal plugin gets wrong. We get it right because we model the actual circuit interaction between your guitar’s output impedance and the pedal’s input stage.

It responds like the real thing.

Pick dynamics, string attack, volume knob position—the circuit responds to all of it because it’s processing signal through simulated components, not looking up a pre-baked response curve. MONSOON’s repeats degrade naturally. RATKING’s filter sweeps interact with the gain stage. The feel is the physics.

It can be modded like the real thing.

Open circuit view. Swap diode types in GOLDENROD. Change transistors in FIZZ. Push MONSOON’s BBD bias out of spec. Every mod that exists on a forum thread, available in one click—plus mods that would destroy the original hardware. Your pedal. Your circuit. Your rules.

The Lineup

{{/* GOLDENROD */}}

GOLDENROD

“The pedal that started a religion.”

Transparent Overdrive · Clean Boost · Edge of Breakup

Dual germanium diode clipping, clean blend circuit, charge pump voltage doubler. The full gain-knob interaction that captures and clones flatten into a single snapshot.

{{/* RATKING */}}
RATKING

“The filter is the voice.”

Distortion · Fuzz-Adjacent Saturation · Shoegaze Wall

LM308 op-amp hard clipping into the legendary passive filter network. First published WDF model of this circuit—the filter-gain interaction solved from the schematic.

{{/* MONSOON */}}
MONSOON

“Every repeat, a little more gone.”

Analog Delay · Modulated Ambience · Oscillating Feedback

Hybrid architecture. Analog signal path in pure WDF. BBD chip modeled with a matched DSP kernel that captures clock feedthrough, companding artifacts, and per-stage high-frequency rolloff.

{{/* FIZZ */}}
FIZZ

“Germanium is alive.”

Germanium Fuzz · Velcro Sag · Violin-Like Sustain

Two PNP germanium transistors sharing a feedback network. Bias points shift with input signal, temperature, and your guitar’s impedance. The first plugin where the fuzz actually cleans up when you roll back the volume.

{{/* SHRINE */}}
SHRINE

“Spring reverb without the spring.”

Spring Reverb · Hall · Shimmer

Hybrid architecture. Analog I/O conditioning in WDF. Reverb core models the allpass/comb filter network with parameters derived from the actual module spec. The specific character of the original, not generic spring reverb.
